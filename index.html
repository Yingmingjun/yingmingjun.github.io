Biography

I am a final-year undergraduate at Chongqing University of Posts and Telecommunications, supervised by Prof. Yongjun Xu, and my thesis is about Robust Resource Allocation Algorithm Based on Backscatter Communication. I am currently applying for a Ph.D. in Wireless Communication (Fall 2023). My motivation to study wireless communication is facilitate access for the global population, providing opportunities regardless of age, gender, race, ethnicity, religion, disability or any other distinguishing characteristics.

The year 2019 marked a crucial transition in my life. On a personal level, I had just become a university student, but more importantly, it was the year when the 5G first hit the ground running in China, opening an unprecedented new era where the limitless potential of this new technology would enable everybody and everything on our planet to be always connected. However, this futuristic vision will only be possible if the 5G can be deployed successfully in an affordable manner. That is why academia has been exploring cutting-edge solutions to resource allocation problems to lower costs and bring the fully connected world to life for all potential users. From a personal perspective, I would embrace the opportunity to participate in the development of B5G/6G technology.

Research

My current research interests lie in the fields of Resource Allcation, Reconfigurable Intelligent Surfaces, Backscatter Communication, Channel estimation, MIMO, etc.

Projects

Consumption Factor Theory (Jan. 2023)

  • Supervisor: Prof. Theodore S. Rappaport (Founding Director of NYU WIRELESS, IEEE Fellow)
  • The Consumption Factor (CF) theory aims to provide engineers with a quantitative method for comparing and analyzing the energy efficiency of different systems, thus making it easier to identify the most efficient options.
  • This research aims to provide a brief overview of recent results in this field. The CF theory for cascaded communication systems is studied. Furthermore, we study the CF theory for future mmWave and sub-THz systems, which demonstrates great feasibility for future wireless communication systems.

Massive MIMO, mmWave MIMO, Spatial Modulation (Dec. 2022)

  • Supervisor: Prof. Aditya K. Jagannatham (Professor of IIT Kanpur)
  • This is a unique cutting edge project that will feature exhaustive lecture modules and several PYTHON/ MATLAB projects/ case-studies on the latest MIMO, Massive MIMO, mmWave MIMO, NOMA, Cooperative, OFDM, and FBMC technologies for 5G.
  • I will be able to gain in-depth knowledge of PYTHON/ MATLAB programming and practical hands-on experience of working on state-of-the-art 5G projects.

Frequency Allocation for 5G+: The Magic of Mobile Communications (Jul. 2021 - Aug. 2021)

  • Supervisor: Prof. Kai-Kit Wong (IEEE Fellow), UCL
  • Research into theoretical and practical aspects of wireless communication systems with an emphasis on frequency and power allocation problems.

Undergraduate Research Training Program of CQUPT (Apr. 2021 - Apr. 2022)
